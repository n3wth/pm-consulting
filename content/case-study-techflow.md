# Case Study: How We Helped a Series B Startup Fix Their Broken Roadmap (And Increase Revenue 40%)

**Company:** TechFlow (Series B fintech, $8M ARR)  
**Problem:** Roadmap misalignment, slow shipping, conflicting stakeholder priorities  
**Timeline:** 4-month engagement  
**Result:** Revenue increased 40%, team velocity +60%, stakeholder satisfaction 9.2/10

## The Situation

TechFlow had product-market fit. They were growing fast. But they were chaotic.

Their CEO wanted growth features. Engineering wanted to refactor technical debt. Sales wanted a specific feature for their top customer. Customer Success wanted improvements to the onboarding flow.

Nobody agreed on what to build next.

The result: 
- Features shipped slowly (10 weeks for something that should take 4)
- Stakeholders were constantly frustrated
- The team wasn't motivated (building things that felt arbitrary)
- Revenue growth had plateaued

They hired us to fix it.

## Our Audit (Week 1-2)

We ran stakeholder interviews and found:

1. **No shared strategy.** Everyone had a different idea of what the product should be in 12 months.
2. **No shared metrics.** CEO measured by revenue. Engineering measured by technical debt reduction. Sales measured by customer retention. No common language.
3. **No communication framework.** Decisions happened in Slack. Reversals happened in meetings. Nobody knew what was actually committed.
4. **Engineering didn't trust product.** They felt like product was "throwing features at the wall."

This is incredibly common in Series B companies.

## The Framework We Built (Week 3-4)

### Step 1: Define Shared Strategy (2 days)

We held a 2-day strategy sprint with CEO, CTO, Head of Sales, Head of CS.

**The output:**
- **12-month vision:** "TechFlow becomes the #1 choice for mid-market fintech companies because we solve fraud detection better than anyone (problem solved: false positives down 90%)"
- **6-month themes:** 
  - Q1-Q2: Build fraud detection suite (main revenue driver)
  - Q3: Expand API ecosystem (growth for advanced customers)
  - Q4: Improve onboarding (reduce churn from 8% to 6%)

Every decision from then on was evaluated against these themes.

### Step 3: Build the Roadmap (Week 5)

We created a quarterly roadmap aligned to the strategy:

**Q1 Goals (Jan-Mar):**
1. **Ship fraud detection MVP** (Revenue goal | Owner: Eng)
   - Machine learning model
   - Integration with payment processor
   - Dashboard for merchants
   - Success metric: 94%+ accuracy on test set

2. **Improve onboarding flow** (Retention goal | Owner: Product/Design)
   - New welcome sequence
   - In-app contextual help
   - Video walkthroughs
   - Success metric: 7-day completion <2 days (vs current 14 days)

3. **Technical refactor phase 1** (Platform health | Owner: CTO)
   - Upgrade database layer
   - Improve API response times
   - Success metric: API p99 latency <200ms

**Q2 Goals (Apr-Jun):**
1. **Launch API marketplace** (Growth goal)
2. **Expand fraud detection** (Revenue goal)
3. **Implement customer health scoring** (Retention goal)

This wasn't 30 initiatives. It was 3 per quarter. Focused.

### Step 4: Create Accountability Framework

Every goal had:
- **Single owner** (not "co-owned")
- **Clear success metrics** (not "we'll know it when we see it")
- **Dependencies called out** (if X blocks Y)
- **Weekly status** (green/yellow/red)

The CTO owned technical refactor. Product owned onboarding. Engineering lead owned fraud detection. Everyone knew their lane.

### Step 5: Build Communication System

**Weekly (Every Monday):**
- Standup email (2 min read): What shipped, what's blocked, what's coming
- Shared dashboard updated (automated)

**Monthly (First Tuesday):**
- 1-hour stakeholder sync: Review metrics, discuss blockers, celebrate wins

**Quarterly (End of quarter):**
- 3-day roadmap review (metrics review, customer feedback, next quarter planning)

## The Results (4 Months In)

### Team Morale
Before: "Why are we building this?" Frustrated.  
After: "Here's why this matters to the business." Motivated.

## Key Lessons From This Engagement

1. **Shared metrics change behavior.** When engineering saw that faster feature velocity helped sales close deals, and that helped revenue, it aligned naturally.

2. **Clear ownership prevents gridlock.** When one person owned each goal, decisions got made faster. Less committee.

3. **Communication > complexity.** They didn't need complex tools. They needed a system: weekly updates, monthly syncs, quarterly planning. Done.

4. **Focus is a competitive advantage.** Saying no to 20 ideas so they could say yes to 3 created 40% revenue growth. Focus wins.

5. **Trust builds incrementally.** Month 1, they were skeptical. Month 2, cautiously optimistic. Month 3, fully aligned. It takes time.
