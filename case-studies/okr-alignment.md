# Case Study: Implementing OKRs to Align a Distributed Product Team

**Client:** CloudScale (Infrastructure SaaS) | **Timeline:** 4 months | **Team Size:** 18 engineers, 4 PMs | **Industry:** DevOps

## The Challenge

CloudScale had grown to 100 employees across 3 time zones (San Francisco, Dublin, Singapore). The product team was fragmented—each PM had a different roadmap, different priorities, different definitions of success.

**The symptoms:**
- Cross-team dependencies weren't aligned (Engineering finished their work; waiting on Design's work 2 weeks earlier)
- Sales was confused about product direction ("What's the vision?")
- Customer advisory board meetings surfaced conflicting promises
- Churn spiked to 12% (industry: 6%)

**Root cause:** No shared vision. Each PM was optimizing locally instead of globally.

---

## The Approach

We implemented OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) to create company-wide alignment.

### Phase 1: OKR Framework Training (2 weeks)

First, we got executive buy-in with a clear definition:

**Objective:** What do we want to achieve? (qualitative)
- Example: "Become the easiest cloud infrastructure platform to use"

**Key Results:** How will we know we've achieved it? (quantitative)
- Example: "Reduce setup time from 4 hours to 30 minutes"
- Example: "Achieve 70% feature adoption rate"
- Example: "Hit NPS of 50+"

We held a 2-day workshop with the leadership team to define Q1 company OKRs:

**Company OKRs (Q1):**
1. **O1: Establish CloudScale as the easiest cloud infra to use**
   - KR1: Reduce setup time to <30 minutes (was 4 hours)
   - KR2: Achieve 70% feature adoption (was 55%)
   - KR3: Hit NPS of 50+ (was 42)

2. **O2: Reduce churn below industry benchmark**
   - KR1: Reduce overall churn to <6% (was 12%)
   - KR2: <5% churn for mid-market segment
   - KR3: Increase customer lifetime value by 15%

3. **O3: Build scalable product operations**
   - KR1: Launch quarterly planning cadence
   - KR2: Achieve 90% on-time shipment vs. roadmap
   - KR3: Reduce technical debt backlog by 25%

### Phase 2: Department-Level OKRs (1 week)

Each PM then drafted OKRs for their team, tied to company OKRs.

**Product & Design OKRs:**
- Support O1 by shipping onboarding redesign
- Support O2 by building retention features (guided tours, re-engagement)
- Support O3 by establishing roadmap discipline

**Engineering OKRs:**
- Support O1 by making core flows 3x faster
- Support O2 by fixing top 10 customer pain points
- Support O3 by reducing critical technical debt

**Sales & Marketing OKRs:**
- Support O1 by teaching product-first positioning
- Support O2 by ensuring customer success touchpoints during onboarding
- Support O3 (N/A—sales/mktg supporting ops indirectly)

### Phase 3: Initiative Planning (2 weeks)

For each OKR, we defined the initiatives (projects) that would drive results.

**Example:** Support O1 KR1 (Setup time <30 min)

Initiatives:
- Initiative A: Redesign onboarding UI (4 weeks, 3 engineers + 1 designer)
- Initiative B: Auto-provision infrastructure (3 weeks, 2 engineers)
- Initiative C: Create video tutorials (2 weeks, marketing + PM)

We scored each initiative by impact, effort, and confidence. Then we committed to a realistic set.

### Phase 4: Cadence & Accountability (ongoing, 3 months)

**Weekly Check-ins (30 min):**
Each team reports on progress:
- Status: On track, at risk, off track
- Progress toward KRs
- Blockers
- Required support

**Monthly All-Hands (60 min):**
Company-wide update on OKR progress. Celebration of wins. Course correction.

**Quarterly Review (2 hours):**
Deep dive on results:
- Did we hit KRs? (Expected: 60-70% hit rate is good)
- What accelerated progress?
- What blocked progress?
- Lessons learned?

## Key Learnings

### 1. OKRs Force Clarity

Before OKRs: "Let's improve onboarding" (vague)
After OKRs: "Reduce setup time to <30 min, measure by tracking first-install time" (specific)

Clarity accelerates execution.

### 2. Ambitious Goals Unlock Creativity

We set KR1 to <30 min (down from 4 hours). Engineers said, "That's impossible."

Then they shipped:
- Auto-provisioning infrastructure (engineering solution)
- Video tutorials (product solution)
- Pre-built environments (product solution)

Impossible goals unlock innovation.

### 3. The 60-70% Hit Rate is Healthy

We hit 78% of OKRs in Q1. This is actually above target. Why?
- Too many teams played it safe (sandbagging)
- A few OKRs were genuinely unrealistic

In Q2, we encouraged slightly more ambition. Result: 65% hit rate, but bigger breakthroughs.
