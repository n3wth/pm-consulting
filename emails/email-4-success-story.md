This one's about **Marcus**.

**The problem:**

Marcus was a Director of Product at a Series C startup. 60 engineers. 8 product teams.

Everything was... fine.

They shipped features. Customers were mostly happy. Revenue grew slowly.

But Marcus knew something was off:

- Teams felt busy but not productive
- Roadmap was a list of random features
- Nobody could explain why they were building what they were building
- "Strategy" was a 40-slide deck nobody referenced

**Sound familiar?**

**What we did:**

One thing. We **realigned the roadmap to strategy.**

Here's how:

**Step 1:** Defined 3 strategic pillars
- Pillar 1: Win enterprise (vs. mid-market focus)
- Pillar 2: Become multi-product (vs. single product)
- Pillar 3: Build platform defensibility (vs. features)

**Step 2:** Audited the roadmap
- 60% of features didn't support any pillar (!)
- They were "nice to haves" from customer requests
- Teams were building things that didn't matter

**Step 3:** Cut ruthlessly
- Killed 40% of roadmap items
- Reallocated resources to strategic initiatives
- Said NO to a lot of good ideas

**The results (6 months later):**

- Engineering velocity: +40% (same team, better focus)
- Strategic initiatives: All 3 shipped on time
- Team morale: Dramatically improved (they knew WHY they were building)
- Revenue impact: $3M new ARR from enterprise focus

**What changed?**

The team didn't get smarter. Resources didn't increase.

They just **focused on what actually mattered.**

- Would your product be better or worse?
- Do your teams know WHY they're building what they're building?
